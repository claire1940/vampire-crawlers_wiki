## The Foundation: Skill Mastery vs. Skill Evolution

Before you can even think about evolving a skill, you must first master it. In Tensura Incremental, mastery represents your character's proficiency with a specific ability. This is tracked via a percentage bar that fills as you use the skill or train it through various in-game activities. 

A common misconception among the player base is that reaching 100% mastery will automatically trigger an evolution. While 100% mastery is a mandatory prerequisite, it is only one half of the equation. You can have a maxed-out mastery bar, but without the correct racial tier, the skill will remain in its basic form. This distinction is vital for efficient gameplay, as it prevents players from over-investing in skills that are currently capped by their racial evolution.

When you perform a race upgrade, you aren't just changing your appearance or base stats; you are essentially unlocking a new "tier" of the game's logic. If you have mastered a skill to 100%, that skill is essentially "waiting" for the container to expand. The moment you click that race upgrade button, the mastery is consumed (or converted), and the skill transforms. This is the secret to **tensura: incremental how to evolve skills** that many players miss because they are looking for a specific skill-evolution button that simply doesn't exist.
