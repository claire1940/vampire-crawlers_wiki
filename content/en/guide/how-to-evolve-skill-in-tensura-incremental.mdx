## The Core Mechanics of Skill Evolution

In Tensura Incremental, skills are the backbone of your combat effectiveness and resource generation. Unlike traditional RPGs where you might simply spend "skill points" to level up an ability, this game utilizes a dual-requirement system. You cannot simply jump to a higher tier of power without first proving your proficiency with your current kit.

The first pillar of this system is **Skill Mastery**. Every time you use an ability or let it run in the background (as is common in incremental games), you gain mastery points. These points contribute to a percentage bar visible in your skills menu. You must reach 100% mastery before the game even considers you eligible for an upgrade.

The second pillar is **Race Evolution**. This is where most players get confused. You don't "evolve" a skill in a vacuum; instead, your skills evolve as a consequence of your character evolving into a higher racial tier. For example, moving from a "Slime" to a "Demon Slime" triggers a transformation of your base abilities into their "Ultimate" or "Extra" versions.

| Requirement | Purpose | How to Increase |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Skill Mastery** | Unlocks the potential for evolution | Continuous use of the skill or AFK training |
| **Race Tier** | Acts as the trigger for the actual evolution | Spending accumulated essence or souls in the Race menu |
| **Soul Count** | Resource required for the evolution process | Defeating enemies in dungeons or completing quests |

> **Tip:** Do not ignore your mastery bar. Even if you have the resources to evolve your race, your skills will remain in their base forms if they haven't reached 100% mastery first.

## Step-by-Step: How to Evolve Skill in Tensura Incremental

If you are looking for a streamlined path to power, follow these specific steps. This process ensures that you are always operating at peak efficiency without getting stuck on technical requirements.

1.  **Select Your Primary Skill**: Focus on one or two skills at a time. Spreading your focus too thin can slow down your mastery gains.
2.  **Grind to 100% Mastery**: Leave your character training or use the skills actively in the early-game dungeons. Check the "Skills" tab frequently to monitor progress.

4.  **Execute the Race Evolution**: Once mastery is at 100% and materials are ready, click the Evolve button in the Race menu.
5.  **Verify the Skill Change**: Your skill names should change (e.g., "Water Blade" becoming "Water Spirit Wave") and their damage multipliers will significantly increase.

## Common Misconceptions and Troubleshooting

Many players believe they need to max out every single skill in their inventory before they can progress their race. This is a common myth that stems from early versions of the game. In the modern version of Tensura Incremental, you only need to reach 100% mastery on the skills you actually want to evolve.

If your skills are not evolving despite meeting the requirements, consider the following:
*   **The Bug Factor**: Occasionally, the UI may not update immediately. Try re-logging into the server after performing a Race Evolution.
*   **Prestige Resets**: When you prestige, your mastery may reset depending on your current "Keep Mastery" perks. Always check your prestige shop before resetting.
*   **Hidden Requirements**: Some "Ultimate" skills require you to have specific items equipped during the evolution process.

## FAQ

**Q: How do I check my current skill mastery percentage?**

A: Open the "Skills" menu from the sidebar. Each skill icon will have a progress bar underneath it or a percentage listed in the detailed tooltip when hovered over or clicked.

**Q: Can I evolve a skill without evolving my race?**

A: No. In the current version of the game, the actual evolution of a skill (changing its name and tier) is tied directly to the Race Evolution mechanic. You must evolve your race to trigger the skill upgrade.

**Q: What is the fastest way to learn how to evolve skill in tensura incremental for beginners?**

A: The fastest way is to focus on a single race evolution at a time. Don't try to master every skill you unlock; pick the strongest one, get it to 100%, and then immediately push for your next race tier using dungeon-farmed souls.

**Q: Does skill mastery reset after I evolve?**

A: Generally, no. Once a skill has evolved, it starts a new mastery track for its higher-tier version. However, performing a "Prestige" might reset these values unless you have purchased the specific "Mastery Retention" upgrades in the prestige shop.
