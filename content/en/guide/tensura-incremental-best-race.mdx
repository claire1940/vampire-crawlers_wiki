## Why Race Selection Dictates Your Progression

In Tensura Incremental, your race is not just a cosmetic choice. It acts as the foundation for your entire build. Unlike traditional RPGs where you might level up stats individually, this game ties your power spikes to racial evolution. To upgrade your skills, you must first reach 100% mastery with your current abilities and then trigger an evolution.

The **tensura incremental best race** for most players is the one that allows for the fastest transition through these evolutionary stages. If a race has high requirements for evolution, you will find yourself stuck in lower-tier zones for much longer, slowing down your soul collection and existence point (EP) gains.

### The Evolution-Skill Connection
Many new players make the mistake of focusing solely on skill mastery. While reaching 100% mastery is a mandatory prerequisite, it does not actually "level up" the skill's potency or tier on its own. Instead, the evolution of your race is the catalyst that transforms your basic techniques into advanced magic and physical arts.

| Evolution Phase | Skill Mastery Required | Resulting Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Base Form** | 0% - 99% | Standard skill damage and cooldowns |
| **Mastery Cap** | **100%** | Unlocks the ability to evolve the race |
| **Post-Evolution** | Reset to 0% | Skills are upgraded to higher tiers/damage |

> **Warning:** Never attempt to evolve before hitting 100% skill mastery. Doing so can result in inefficient stat spreads and may require additional grinding to fix your progression path.

## Understanding Skill Evolution Mechanics

A common point of confusion for players using the **tensura incremental best race** is how skills actually improve. Many expect skills to level up automatically through use, similar to a traditional RPG. However, Tensura Incremental uses a "Mastery then Evolve" system.

When you use a skill, its mastery percentage increases. Once it hits 100%, the skill is "primed." The actual upgrade to the skill's base damage, area of effect, or utility only occurs when your race evolves to the next stage. This creates a gameplay loop where you farm mastery, then farm EP for evolution, and then repeat the process in a more difficult zone.

### Mastery Farming Tips
*   **Target Dummies:** If you are playing solo, use target dummies in the safe zone to build mastery without the risk of dying.
*   **Multi-Hit Skills:** Focus on mastering skills that hit multiple enemies at once, as these typically gain mastery faster in crowded raid scenarios.
*   **Don't Skip Mastery:** If you evolve your race before a skill hits 100% mastery, you may miss out on the upgrade for that specific evolution tier.

The Slime race is the most iconic choice, following the path of Rimuru Tempest. ### The Ogre Race
Ogres are the "bruisers" of Tensura Incremental. They have higher base health and physical defense. This makes them excellent for players who want to solo raids without needing a carry. However, their evolution requirements are stricter than Humans, making them less ideal for fast prestige cycles.

### The Lizardman Race
Often overlooked, the Lizardman race offers unique evolution paths that focus on speed and critical hits. While not currently the **tensura incremental best race** for the general meta, they are highly effective in specific dungeon encounters where mobility is more important than raw health.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the absolute best race for a beginner in Tensura Incremental?**

A: The Human race is widely considered the **tensura incremental best race** for beginners. It has the simplest evolution requirements and allows you to reach your first prestige much faster than any other starting race.

**Q: Why aren't my skills leveling up even though I'm using them constantly?**

A: In Tensura Incremental, skills only upgrade when you evolve your race. You must reach 100% mastery on the skill first, and then perform a racial evolution to see a change in the skill's tier or power.

**Q: How many souls do I need to prestige?**

A: While existence points are the main requirement for evolution, you generally need around 10,000 souls to progress through the necessary gates leading up to a prestige. Farming raids like Falmouth is the most efficient way to gather these.

**Q: Is the Slime race better than the Human race for the end-game?**

A: The Slime race eventually reaches higher power ceilings as a Demon Slime, but the journey takes significantly longer. If your goal is to climb the leaderboards through multiple prestiges, the Human race's speed makes it more efficient in the long run.
