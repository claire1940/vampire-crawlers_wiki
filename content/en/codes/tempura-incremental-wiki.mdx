## Getting Started: The Basics of Frying

The initial phase of the game is focused on manual clicking and basic resource management. You begin with a modest deep fryer and a supply of shrimp. Every click represents a piece of tempura being prepared. The quality of your output is determined by the "Crispiness Factor," which is a multiplier applied to the base price of the ingredient.

To maximize your early-game income, you must pay close attention to the Heat Meter. Frying at too low a temperature results in soggy, low-value food, while excessive heat leads to burnt ingredients that provide zero profit. Finding the "Golden Zone" is the first skill any chef should master.

## Mastering the Tempura Incremental Wiki: Advanced Upgrades

Once you have established a steady flow of cash, the game shifts from manual clicking to system optimization. The upgrade menu is divided into three primary categories: Batter, Oil, and Kitchen Infrastructure. According to the current **tempura incremental wiki** meta, balancing these three is significantly more effective than maxing out one at a time.

### Batter and Coating
Batter upgrades improve the base multiplier of every item fried. Higher-tier flour and chilled water additives ensure that the tempura remains light and airy, which customers pay a premium for.

### Oil Management
Oil is the lifeblood of your kitchen. Over time, oil degrades, reducing the "Golden Zone" on your heat meter. Investing in high-grade vegetable oils or traditional sesame oil blends will increase the duration before oil changes are required and improve the flavor profile (profit multiplier) of your food.

### Kitchen Infrastructure
This category includes automation tools like the Auto-Dipper and the Conveyor Belt. These allow you to generate income while the game is running in the background, a staple feature of any incremental experience.

## The Renovation System: Prestige Mechanics

Like many idle games, progress eventually slows down as upgrade costs outpace your current earning potential. This is where the Renovation system (the game's prestige mechanic) comes into play. By choosing to "Renovate" your restaurant, you reset your current cash, ingredients, and basic upgrades in exchange for **Chef Stars**.

Chef Stars provide a permanent, non-resetting boost to your kitchen's overall productivity. When consulting this **tempura incremental wiki** for prestige timing, most top-tier players suggest waiting until you can at least triple your current Chef Star count before performing a Renovation.

| Chef Star Milestone | Permanent Unlock | Bonus Description |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **10 Stars** | Secret Spice | +50% Base Value to all items |
| **50 Stars** | Master Fryer | Unlocks "Flash Fry" ability |
| **250 Stars** | Golden Shrimp | 1% chance for 100x profit items |
| **1,000 Stars** | Michelin Star | Global 5x multiplier to all income |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not renovate immediately upon reaching the milestone. Push for an extra 10-15% of stars to ensure your "recovery" back to your previous peak is fast and efficient.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the most important stat in Tempura Incremental?**

A: While all stats matter, "Crispiness" is generally considered the most important because it acts as a final multiplier on your total earnings. High base value ingredients are useless if your crispiness multiplier is low.

**Q: How often should I change my oil?**

A: According to the **tempura incremental wiki** data, you should change your oil as soon as the "Golden Zone" on your heat meter shrinks to less than 20% of the total bar. Frying in dirty oil significantly hampers your profit margins.

**Q: Is it better to focus on one ingredient or unlock them all?**

A: It is better to focus on your highest-unlocked ingredient. However, you should still purchase the basic upgrades for lower-tier ingredients to benefit from the "Menu Synergy" bonuses that unlock every 50 total levels across all items.

**Q: What are Golden Shrimp, and how do I get them?**

A: Golden Shrimp are rare spawns that occur once you have invested in the "Golden Flour" prestige upgrade. They provide a massive injection of cash and a temporary 2x speed boost to your entire kitchen.
