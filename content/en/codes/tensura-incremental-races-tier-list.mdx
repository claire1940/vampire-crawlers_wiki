## S-Tier: The Pinnacle of Power

The S-Tier is reserved for races that have achieved their "True" status. In the 2026 meta, these races are the gold standard for anyone looking to top the leaderboards or clear the hardest dungeons.

### True Demon Lord (Slime Evolution Path)
The Slime path remains one of the most popular choices due to its versatility. Once you reach the "Demon Slime" stage and fulfill the requirements for Harvest Festival, you can evolve into a True Demon Lord. This race offers a massive boost to magicule capacity and skill cooldown reduction.

### True Hero (Human Evolution Path)
Often overlooked in the early game, the Human race scales incredibly well. By following the Hero path and achieving "Chosen Hero" status, players gain access to unique light-based abilities and high physical defense, making them nearly unkillable in PvP scenarios.

### Dragonoid
Dragonoids are the raw powerhouses of Tensura Incremental. While they have fewer utility skills compared to Slimes, their base ATK multiplier is the highest in the game. If your goal is to one-shot bosses and speed through prestige cycles, this is the race to aim for.

## A-Tier: Competitive Mid-Game Options

A-Tier races are highly effective and can often carry a player through the majority of the game's content. They are generally easier to obtain than S-Tier races but slightly less efficient at the absolute limit of progression.

## B-Tier and C-Tier: The Foundation

While you won't stay in these tiers for long, choosing the right starting point can shave hours off your initial climb. 

*   **Humans (B-Tier):** Great for players who want a steady, predictable path. They have access to a wide variety of equipment.
*   **Goblins (C-Tier):** Though weak, they evolve into Hobgoblins very quickly. This makes them excellent for players who want to see immediate progress.
*   **Lizardmen (C-Tier):** These offer a balance between the physical prowess of Orcs and the speed of Goblins. Their evolution into Dragonewts is a solid mid-game goal.

> **Warning:** Avoid staying as a basic Slime for too long. While the end-game Slime path is S-Tier, the initial "Bottom Slime" phase is the weakest in the game. Evolve into a "Cloud Slime" or "Water Slime" as soon as the option becomes available.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I change my race after I have started evolving?**

A: Yes, but it typically requires a "Rebirth Stone" or a specific prestige reset. When you change races, you will keep your permanent prestige bonuses, but you will have to start from the base form of the new race.

**Q: What is the best race for a completely F2P (Free to Play) player?**

A: The Slime path is generally considered the best for F2P players. It offers a very smooth progression curve and doesn't rely as heavily on premium equipment as the Human or Dragonoid paths do.

**Q: How often does the tensura incremental races tier list change?**

A: The meta usually shifts with every major "Season" or "World" update. In 2026, we have seen two major shifts due to the introduction of "Ultimate Skills" which benefit magic-based races more than physical ones.

**Q: Do I need to max all skills before I evolve my race?**

A: No. As mentioned in the prestige guides, you only need to focus on maxing your race upgrade. Maxing every skill is a waste of time in the early and mid-game; save the completionist grinding for when you reach your final S-Tier form.
