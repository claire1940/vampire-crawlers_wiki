## Understanding Roll Types and Rewards

In Tensura Incremental, progression is tied to three primary categories of "Rolls." Understanding what each one does will help you decide when to use your codes and which builds to focus on.

### Race Rolls
Race Rolls allow you to change your character's biological species. In the world of Tensura, your race determines your base stat growth, elemental resistances, and evolution potential. Starting as a Slime is standard, but rolling into an Ogre, Kijin, or even a rare Dragonoid can significantly boost your combat effectiveness.

### Skill Rolls
Skills are the bread and butter of your offensive and defensive capabilities. Based on the anime's "Unique Skills" system, these rolls can grant you abilities like "Gluttony," which allows you to devour enemies for permanent stat gains, or "Auto-Battle," which streamlines the incremental grind.

### Spirit Rolls
Spirit Rolls are used to enhance your character's spiritual pressure and mana efficiency. These are particularly useful for players who focus on magic-based builds or those looking to survive the deeper, more hazardous floors of the Dungeons.

| **Skill Roll** | **Unique Abilities** | Mid-game boss hunting |

## Strategy for Using Your Codes

It is tempting to use all your rolls as soon as you redeem them, but a strategic approach can lead to much better results. 

> **Tip:** Save your Skill Rolls until you have evolved at least once. Some high-tier skills have synergy with specific evolved races, and rolling them early might lock you into a build that doesn't fit your endgame goals.

If you are a new player, focus on using **Race Rolls** first. Landing a high-tier race like a High Orc or a Greater Spirit will make the initial grind through the cave much faster. Once you can comfortably clear the first few dungeon floors, start using your **Skill Rolls** to find abilities that offer area-of-effect (AoE) damage, as this will speed up your devouring rate.
