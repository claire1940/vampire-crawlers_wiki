## Core Gameplay Mechanics: A New Perspective

While *Vampire Survivors* was defined by its "bullet heaven" chaos, *Vampire Crawlers* focuses on deliberate, turn-based card combat. You navigate randomized dungeons in a first-person view, moving one square at a time. The world is populated by 2D sprites in a 3D environment, creating a charming "boomer shooter" aesthetic applied to the RPG genre.

### The Deck-Building Combat System
Combat triggers when you encounter enemies in the dungeon. Unlike traditional RPGs, your actions are dictated by a deck of cards. These cards represent the iconic weapons from the series:
*   **Knife:** Targeted high damage to a single lane.
*   **Garlic:** Low damage but creates a defensive aura.
*   **King Bible:** Orbits the player, damaging enemies that get too close.
*   **Axe:** High area-of-effect damage that can hit multiple lanes.

Enemies approach through five distinct lanes. Your goal is to eliminate them before they reach your position and deplete your health pool. You have a set amount of mana each turn, but through clever combos, you can extend your turn almost indefinitely.

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Pay attention to the sequential mana cost of your cards. Playing a 0-cost, then a 1-cost, then a 2-cost card triggers a combo multiplier that increases the effectiveness of every subsequent card played in that chain.

## Characters and Progression: The Crawler System

The game features 21 unique "Crawlers," each acting as a character class with its own starting deck and passive abilities. Unlocking these characters requires gold earned during runs, mirroring the progression system of the original game.

| Crawler Name | Starting Weapon | Unique Passive |
|---|---|---|
| **Gennaro** | Knife | +1 Projectile to all attack cards |
| **Imelda** | Magic Wand | Gains +5% XP every 5 cards played |
| **Arca** | Fire Wand | Reduces cooldown/mana cost of fire cards |
| **Porta** | Lightning Ring | Starts with permanent +30% Area |

Beyond the characters, the permanent upgrade shop returns with 21 different categories. Players can invest gold into stats like **Might**, **Luck**, **Hand Size**, and **Max Mana**. These upgrades are vital because the difficulty scaling in *Vampire Crawlers* is notoriously steep. Bosses do not wait for your turn to end; they can interrupt your combos every few cards played, forcing you to balance offense with defensive cards like Armor or Freeze.

## Advanced Strategies for Dungeon Exploration

Exploring the 15 different biomes requires more than just a good deck; it requires environmental awareness. Dungeons are filled with destructible objects, hidden chests, and NPCs like the Blacksmith.

### Managing Card Durability
One of the most punishing mechanics in the game is "Card Shattering." If you use the same card too many times within a single encounter to create an infinite loop, the card will begin to crack. Continuous abuse leads to the card being permanently destroyed for the remainder of the run. 

If you push the system too far, the **Grim Reaper** will appear mid-combat. Unlike previous games where the Reaper signifies the end of a timed run, here he acts as a "combo-breaker" boss that you must defeat to continue. If you stall for too long, the dungeon may even begin to fill with water, creating a secondary "timer" that forces you to finish the fight or face a watery grave.

### The Village Hub
Between runs, you return to The Village. This serves as your primary hub for:
1.  **Permanent Upgrades:** Spend gold to increase base stats.
2.  **The Blacksmith:** Add gems to your cards to give them new properties (e.g., "This card is always free").
3.  **Arcana Selection:** Equip powerful modifiers that change the rules of the dungeon.
4.  **Achievement Tracking:** With 161 achievements, there is always a new goal to strive for.
