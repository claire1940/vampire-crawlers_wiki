## Understanding Vampire Crawlers Luck and Stats

In the early stages of a run, your primary goal is to stabilize your defense and increase your resource gain. Luck in this game isn't just a hidden number; it is a tangible stat increased by items like the Clover. Higher luck increases the quality of card rewards upon leveling up and improves the loot found in breakable objects like chests and candelabras.

| Item/Stat | Primary Effect | Strategy Tip |
|---|---|---|
| **Clover** | Increases **vampire crawlers luck** | Grab this early to ensure better card drafts in later floors. |
| **Spinach** | Boosts raw damage | Essential for clearing the first few waves of Mudmen. |
| **Hollow Heart** | Increases Maximum Health | Play these immediately as they exhaust and don't bloat your deck. |

## The Emldda Experience Engine

Choosing the right character is the foundation of any "broken" run. While Antonio offers a straightforward playstyle, Emldda (the Wizard) is the undisputed queen of scaling. Her starting deck is designed for card draw and mana generation, which allows her to exploit the experience system.

### How to Farm Infinite XP
By utilizing the armor perk purchased in the village lobby, Emldda can start every round with enough block to negate all damage from low-level enemies. This creates a "safe zone" where you can stop attacking and start farming.

1.  **Engage a weak enemy group:** Keep at least two enemies alive.
2.  **Play the Meldder Crawler card:** This grants a 5% experience growth modifier for every yellow card played.
3.  **Cycle through your deck:** Use Attract Orbs to draw cards from your discard pile back into your hand.
4.  **Repeat the loop:** Do not kill the enemies until you have reached level 10 or higher on the very first floor.

## Weapon Evolution and the Pichon Meta

While many weapons like the Death Spiral or Holy Wand are effective, the **Pichon** (the white pigeon) is the most powerful weapon in the 2026 meta. Although it costs 3 mana, its damage scales exponentially with the "Amount" statistic.

### Scaling the Amount Stat
The "Amount" stat determines how many projectiles or instances of damage a card produces. By slotting a **Mount Gem** into a Weighty Tome or Ancient Tome, you can increase your Amount stat every time you play that book. 

*   **Step 1:** Play the Weighty Tome repeatedly using Attract Orbs to draw it back.
*   **Step 2:** Watch your Amount stat climb from 1 to 20+.
*   **Step 3:** Deploy the Pichon. With a high Amount stat, a single pigeon can deal over 1,000,000 damage, instantly evaporating bosses.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Playing the same card more than five times in a single turn will cause the card frame to "crack." This is a developer-inserted mechanic that summons Death if you continue to abuse the loop. Move on to your next card before the fifth activation!

## Surviving the Final Encounter

In the final level of the 2026 demo, you will face upgraded versions of every enemy. The ultimate challenge isn't just the floor boss, but the encounter with Death himself. Death deals 100 damage in a single strike, which is usually a game-over.

To survive, you must stack your armor using the **Hero's Armor** card combined with luck-based buffs. If you can reach over 200 armor and have a health pool higher than 100, you can actually tank a hit from Death. However, it is much more efficient to use your infinite draw engine to find your quadruple-damage Pichon and delete the boss before they can even move.

## FAQ

**Q: How exactly does vampire crawlers luck affect my deck?**

A: Luck influences the "weight" of card rarities during the level-up screen. Higher luck scores make it significantly more likely that you will see Rare (Blue) or Epic (Purple) cards like the Bracer or Pichon early in your run.

**Q: What is the "cracked card" mechanic?**

A: If you play the exact same card instance more than four times in a single turn, the frame will begin to crack. On the fifth play, the game triggers a "Death" event as a way to prevent infinite loops from lasting forever. You can avoid this by having multiple copies of the same card or using "Return" gems wisely.

**Q: Which character is best for beginners?**

A: Antonio is the most beginner-friendly due to his higher starting health and simple physical attacks. However, once you understand the mana system, Emldda is much stronger for long-term progression.

**Q: Can I keep my stats between floors?**

A: Most stats like Might, Amount, and Armor gained during a fight are temporary and reset once the combat ends. However, your character level, maximum health increases from Hollow Hearts, and XP growth modifiers persist throughout the entire run.
