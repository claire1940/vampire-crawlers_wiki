## Understanding Core Vampire Crawlers Stats

When you first open the character selection or pause menu, the list of attributes on the left-hand side can feel overwhelming. These stats represent your character's baseline power before any cards or temporary buffs are applied. Understanding these values is essential for choosing the right character for your playstyle.

| Stat Name | Primary Function | Strategic Importance |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Max Health** | Determines the total damage you can sustain. | Essential for "tank" builds that use health-sacrificing cards. |
| **Recovery** | Percentage of health regained after each successful battle. | High recovery reduces the need for healing items like turkeys. |
| **Hand** | The number of cards drawn at the start of each round. | A larger hand size allows for longer, more complex combos. |
| **Mana** | The resource required to play cards from your hand. | High mana is required to play multiple high-tier cards in one turn. |
| **Armor** | A damage-reduction buffer that resets every round. | Prioritize armor to prevent permanent health loss during minor skirmishes. |
| **Might** | Increases the raw damage output of all offensive abilities. | The primary scaling stat for players focusing on fast kills. |

> **💡 Tip:** Armor is often more valuable than raw Health in the early game because it refreshes every round, acting as a "free" shield that prevents you from needing to find healing items.

## Advanced Combat and Utility Attributes

Beyond the basics, several **vampire crawlers stats** influence the behavior of your projectiles and the efficiency of your resource gathering. These stats often determine how "broken" a build can become during the later floors of a dungeon.

### Projectile and Area Scaling
If you prefer a "bullet heaven" style of play, you must focus on **Area** and **Amount**. Area adds splash damage to your attacks, allowing a single fireball or dagger to hit multiple clustered enemies. **Amount** increases the actual number of projectiles fired. For example, if your base attack fires one projectile, increasing your Amount stat to +1 will double your output, effectively doubling your damage if both shots hit.

### Resource Management
*   **Magnet:** This stat governs your card draw efficiency. If a card has an effect that draws one card, a higher Magnet stat can potentially increase that draw to two or more cards, preventing you from running out of options mid-turn.
*   **Luck:** This is a "hidden" power stat. High Luck increases the frequency of rare dungeon events, such as finding breakable walls that contain permanent stat boosts. It also improves the quality of gems found in treasure chests.
*   **Growth:** This is your XP multiplier. Stacking Growth early in a run allows you to level up much faster, giving you access to more card selections and upgrades before reaching the floor boss.
*   **Greed:** Increases the amount of gold dropped by enemies and found in containers. Use high Greed builds to farm coins for permanent shop upgrades.

## Full Game Exclusive Stats: Roll, Skip, and Banish

As you move from the demo to the full release of 2026, four new **vampire crawlers stats** become vital for deck manipulation. These allow you to control the RNG (randomness) of your level-ups.

| Stat | Function | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Roll** | Allows you to refresh the card selection during a level-up. | Use when none of the offered cards fit your current build. |
| **Skip** | Passes on a level-up selection without adding cards. | Best for keeping your deck small and efficient. |
| **Banish** | Permanently removes a card from the pool for that run. | Use on low-tier cards to increase the odds of seeing legendary cards. |
| **Curse** | Increases enemy difficulty in exchange for massive XP. | Only recommended for veteran players seeking high-score runs. |

## Character-Specific Stat Synergies

Choosing a character is about more than just their starting deck; it’s about how they interact with specific card colors. Each character has a "passive" ability that triggers based on the cards you play, which can drastically alter your effective **vampire crawlers stats** in the heat of battle.

*   **Antonio:** Gains +4 Armor every time his character card is played. Additionally, playing red (damage) cards increases his Might by 10% for the duration of the combat.
*   **Arca:** Focuses on mana regeneration. Playing purple cards restores 1 mana, allowing for nearly infinite loops if paired with low-cost purple spells.
*   **Emldda:** The queen of XP. She stacks Growth stats when yellow cards are played, making her the best choice for players who want to reach level 50+ in a single run.
*   **Pascuina:** Increases her Hand size by +1 whenever a purple card is played. This allows her to hold a massive number of options, making her highly versatile for complex combo chains.

## The Combo System: Scaling Stats Through Order

The core of Vampire Crawlers is the ascending combo system. Most cards have a cost (0, 1, 2, etc.). To maximize your **vampire crawlers stats** during a turn, you should play cards in order.

1.  **Start at Zero:** Playing a 0-cost card "primes" your 1-cost cards.
2.  **The Glow Effect:** Once a combo is active, the next cards in the sequence will glow. Playing a glowing card provides bonus damage, extra buffs, or reduced mana costs.
3.  **Wild Cards:** These special cards can be played at any time to extend a combo without breaking the sequence. They are essential for jumping from a 1-cost card to a 3-cost card if you are missing a 2-cost card in your hand.
4.  **Resetting:** If you play a card out of order, the combo resets to zero, and you lose the multipliers.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful not to make your deck too large. If you have twenty 0-cost cards but no 1-cost cards, you will never be able to trigger the powerful combo finishers that define the late game.

## Meta-Progression and Power-ups

Between runs, you can spend the gold earned via the Greed stat in the Power-up menu. These are permanent increases to your **vampire crawlers stats** that apply to every character you play.

If you are struggling to survive the first floor, follow this upgrade priority:
1.  **Armor & Recovery:** This provides a safety net for beginners.
2.  **Growth (XP Gain):** Faster levels mean more cards and faster power spikes.
3.  **Might & Area:** Once you can survive, you need to be able to clear rooms quickly.
4.  **Crawler Duration:** Increases the number of turns your character's special abilities stay active on the field.

The beauty of this system is that it is "refund-friendly." If you want to try a high-luck build, you can refund your Might points and reinvest them into Luck and Greed at no cost.

## FAQ

**Q: How do temporary buffs differ from permanent vampire crawlers stats?**

A: Permanent stats (shown in the top right) persist across all battles and floors. Temporary buffs (shown in the top left) are usually triggered by "Yellow" cards or dungeon events (like breaking a candle) and only last for the duration of the current battle.

**Q: What is the best stat to focus on for a beginner?**

A: Armor and Recovery are the best for beginners. Armor prevents you from taking chip damage, while Recovery ensures that any mistakes you do make are healed automatically after the fight ends.

**Q: How does the "Banish" mechanic help my stats?**

A: Banish doesn't increase a stat directly, but it improves your "effective stats" by removing weak cards from the rotation. By banishing cards you don't need, you ensure that every card you draw is high-impact, effectively making your Might and Mana more valuable.

**Q: Can I increase my Hand size mid-run?**

A: Yes. Certain characters like Pascuina can increase Hand size through their passive abilities, and specific "Blue" cards or rare dungeon events can permanently add to your Hand stat for the remainder of that run.
