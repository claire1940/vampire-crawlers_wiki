## The Core of Smart Targeting and Area Splash

Most turn-based games suffer from "damage overflow," a situation where a 100-damage attack hits a 5-HP enemy, wasting 95 points of damage. Vampire Crawlers eliminates this frustration. The game utilizes a sophisticated distribution algorithm that calculates the health of the primary target and automatically "splashes" the remaining potency to adjacent or secondary threats.

This **vampire crawlers area splash** system is part of what the developers call "Rush Turns." It allows for a high-speed combat experience where the player doesn't need to manually micro-manage every single strike. The system mimics the feel of manual multi-casting but automates the math so you can focus on strategy rather than resource management.

## FAQ

**Q: Does the vampire crawlers area splash mechanic work on bosses?**

A: Yes, but the behavior changes slightly. If a boss has "adds" (minions), the overflow damage from the boss will splash to the minions. If the boss is alone, the "smart targeting" focus remains entirely on the boss, ensuring no damage is lost.

**Q: Can I turn off the smart targeting if I want to hit a specific enemy?**

A: The system is designed to be "smart" by default to facilitate the "Rush Turn" gameplay. While you still select your primary target, the **vampire crawlers area splash** automatically handles the overflow. There is currently no "manual waste" mode as it would contradict the game's design philosophy of fast-paced combat.

**Q: Do gems that increase damage also increase the splash range?**

A: Indirectly, yes. By increasing the total damage of a card, you increase the amount of "overflow" damage available to be distributed to secondary and tertiary targets. To increase the actual physical range or number of targets, look for specific Evolution or Chain gems.

**Q: Is Vampire Crawlers suitable for players new to deckbuilders?**

A: Absolutely. The **vampire crawlers area splash** system makes the game very approachable because it reduces the penalty for "mis-targeting." Even if a player isn't sure which enemy is the biggest threat, the smart system helps distribute damage effectively, making it easy to pick up and play.
