## Understanding the Incomplete Combustion Mechanic

In the world of Vampire Crawlers, fire damage is categorized into three tiers: Smolder, Complete Combustion, and Incomplete Combustion. While Complete Combustion provides the highest single-target DPS, the **vampire crawlers incomplete combustion** state is preferred for crowd control. It occurs when a fire source is applied to an enemy that is already under a "Suffocation" or "Void" debuff.

Instead of a clean burn, the enemy begins to emit "Soot Clouds." These clouds linger on the battlefield, dealing tick damage to any monster passing through them and reducing their vision range, which effectively prevents ranged enemies from targeting the player.

## FAQ

**Q: Does Incomplete Combustion work on bosses?**

A: Yes, but bosses have higher "Lung Capacity" stats, meaning the soot clouds from **vampire crawlers incomplete combustion** dissipate 50% faster. You will need to re-apply the effect more frequently than you would on standard mobs.

**Q: Can I combine this with Frost damage?**

A: No. Applying Frost damage to an enemy in the **vampire crawlers incomplete combustion** state will trigger "Thermal Shock," which clears the soot clouds and deals a small burst of physical damage. This usually lowers your overall DPS in the long run.

**Q: What is the best character for a vampire crawlers incomplete combustion build?**

**Q: Is Incomplete Combustion affected by the "Fire Damage" stat?**
