## Core Attributes and Stat Definitions

In Vampire Crawlers, stats are more than just passive buffs; they are the engine behind your deck's efficiency. While the game shares the visual language of its predecessor, the mechanical execution is entirely different. Most stats are modified through cards played during an encounter or permanent upgrades purchased between runs.

| Stat Name | Primary Function | Duration |
|---|---|---|
| **Might** | Increases raw damage output for all attack cards. | Current Encounter |
| **Armor** | Mitigates incoming damage from enemy attacks. | Permanent/Current Turn |
| **Mana** | The resource required to play cards from your hand. | Refreshes every turn |
| **Splash Damage** | Deals a percentage of damage to enemies in the back row. | Card Dependent |
| **Luck** | Increases the chances of finding rare gems and extra card options. | Entire Run |

> **💡 Tip:** Might is particularly effective during boss fights. Since it stacks for the duration of the encounter, cycling through low-cost Might-boosting cards early can lead to massive damage numbers in later turns.

## The Influence of the Amount Stat Vampire Crawlers Players Should Know

The **amount stat vampire crawlers** provides is often felt through card duplication and multi-target mechanics. In the original Survivors-like genre, "Amount" typically referred to the number of projectiles. In this first-person deck-builder, that concept has evolved into hand size, card replication, and the "Splash Damage" stat which determines how many rows of enemies your attacks can reach.

For example, certain gems can be slotted into cards to duplicate them upon use. If you have a Mana Tome that provides extra mana for a turn, slotting a duplication gem effectively increases the "amount" of mana you can generate, allowing for explosive turns where you can play your entire hand.

### Mana Sequencing: The Hidden Multiplier
A unique mechanic in this game is "Mana Sequencing." The order in which you play your cards matters just as much as the stats on the cards themselves. Playing a 0-mana card before a 1-mana card can actually boost the effectiveness of the subsequent card. This adds a layer of depth that prevents the game from being a simple "play everything" experience.

## Gem Slotting and Customization

Gems are the primary way to customize the **amount stat vampire crawlers** cards possess. As you progress through a dungeon, you will find chests or level up, granting you the choice of various gems. These are not permanent inventory items but are instead slotted directly into your cards to change their properties.

| Gem Type | Effect on Card | Strategic Use |
|---|---|---|
| **Double Damage** | Multiplies the base damage of the card by two. | Best for 0-mana cards like the Whip. |
| **Leader Gem** | Boosts damage of other cards while this card is held. | Use on high-mana cards you don't intend to play. |
| **Replication** | Creates a copy of the card in your hand after use. | Ideal for Mana-generating or Shield cards. |
| **Might Booster** | Adds a flat Might bonus to the player upon card use. | Perfect for building strength in long boss fights. |

The "Leader Gem" is one of the most strategic additions to the game. It encourages you to keep a high-cost card in your hand rather than discarding it, effectively turning a "dead draw" into a powerful passive buff for your cheaper attacks. This is a radical departure from traditional deck-builders where you want to thin your deck as much as possible.

## Advanced Strategy: Achieving the Flow State

While the game features a "Turbo Wildcard" button (the X button) that allows you to play all cards in your hand at once, relying on this is often a trap. To truly master the **amount stat vampire crawlers** mechanics, you must manually sequence your turns. 

By carefully observing which enemies are targeting you, you can use cards like the King Bible to knock back the front row, effectively canceling their attacks for that turn. This tactical layer is what separates Crawlers from the more "hands-off" experience of previous titles. As you learn the card synergies and the specific effects of items like the 'nduja (which adds "spicy damage"), the game shifts from a puzzle into a rhythmic flow state.

> **Warning:** Be careful with "Spicy Damage" mechanics if your health is low. While powerful, some environmental effects and card synergies can be unpredictable if you haven't fully mapped out your character's resistances.

## FAQ

**Q: What exactly does the amount stat vampire crawlers use do for my character?**

A: In Vampire Crawlers, "Amount" is less of a single numerical stat and more of a category governing card duplication, multi-hit capabilities, and splash damage. Increasing these factors allows you to hit more enemies per turn and cycle through your deck faster.

**Q: Is there a way to increase my mana permanently?**

A: While you can find gems and cards (like the Tome) that increase mana during a run, permanent mana increases are usually tied to specific character unlocks or high-level meta-progression upgrades in the shop.

**Q: How does the King Bible work compared to the original game?**

A: In this version, the King Bible is a card that costs one mana. It deals damage to the entire front row and has a knockback effect. This is vital for defense, as it pushes enemies back a row, forcing them to spend their next turn moving forward instead of attacking you.

**Q: Can I evolve weapons in Vampire Crawlers?**

A: Yes. If you have the correct combination of cards in your deck and meet specific requirements during a run, you can evolve your base weapons into much more powerful versions, mirroring the evolution mechanic from the original survivors-like game.
