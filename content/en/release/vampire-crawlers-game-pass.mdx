## Mastering Card-Based Combat

The heart of the gameplay lies in the card system. Every action—from attacking to healing—is tied to the cards in your hand. To succeed, players must understand the "ascending mana" rule. Playing cards in the correct sequence allows for devastating combos that can clear entire hallways of monsters.

| Combat Action | Mana Cost | Effect | Strategy Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Basic Whip** | 1 | Horizontal strike | Best for clearing low-HP bats early. |
| **Holy Water** | 3 | Area of effect (AOE) burn | Use when surrounded in narrow corridors. |
| **Spinach** | 2 | Damage buff | Always play before your highest damage card. |
| **Wild Card** | Variable | Mimics other cards | Save for completing high-mana combos. |

> **💡 Tip:** Don't just spam your cards as you draw them. Check the turn order and mana availability to ensure you can land a "world-ending combo" on elite enemies.

## Navigation and the Shovel Mechanic

Exploration in Vampire Crawlers is vertical as much as it is horizontal. Unlike traditional crawlers where you simply find a staircase, here you often need to find a **Shovel**. This tool allows you to "dig" into the next terrain, effectively moving you to lower, more difficult floors of the dungeon. 

The dungeons feature "functioning walls" that can be interacted with or destroyed, leading to hidden rooms filled with gems. Gems are a critical resource used to upgrade your cards mid-run, adding permanent stat boosts or reducing mana costs.

### Dungeon Floor Tiers

1.  **The Library:** High visibility, mostly bats and skeletons. Great for building your initial deck.
2.  **The Caves:** Narrower corridors where AOE weapons like Holy Water become essential.
3.  **The Crypt:** Home to zombies and mummies. Requires high-level "Soul" upgrades to survive.

## Deck Building and Evolution

As you progress through a run, you will have the opportunity to "extend your stack." This means adding more cards to your active deck, which increases your versatility but can also dilute your deck's consistency. Finding the right balance is key to surviving the deeper floors.

Weapon evolution also makes a return. By combining specific cards and holding the correct passive items (like Spinach), your basic weapons can evolve into "busted" versions that deal massive damage across multiple tiles. This is often the only way to defeat the "Loot Goblins" that appear randomly, carrying massive amounts of gold and rare gems.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be careful when stacking too many high-mana cards. If you draw a hand full of 4-mana cards and only have 3 mana per turn, you will be a sitting duck for the undead.

## FAQ

**Q: Is Vampire Crawlers a direct sequel to Vampire Survivors?**

A: It is more of a spin-off or a "turbo wild card" entry. While it uses the same assets and characters, the gameplay is entirely different, moving from a real-time action game to a turn-based dungeon crawler.

**Q: How do I unlock new characters in the vampire crawlers game pass version?**

A: Characters are unlocked by finding them in coffins hidden within the dungeons or by purchasing them in the shop using gold earned during your runs. Antonio is usually one of the first major unlocks available.

**Q: What does the Shovel do in the game?**

A: The Shovel is a unique mechanic that allows you to dig through the floor to reach the next level of the dungeon. It is often required to progress when you hit a dead end or want to escape a floor with too many enemies.

**Q: Can I play Vampire Crawlers offline?**

A: Yes, the game supports offline play. However, you will need to connect to the internet periodically to verify your **vampire crawlers game pass** subscription and sync your achievements to the Xbox network.
