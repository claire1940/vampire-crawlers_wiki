## Core Gameplay: The Combo Multiplier System

The heart of the experience lies in how you play your hand. Unlike traditional deckbuilders where you might play cards in any order, this game rewards mathematical precision. You are encouraged to play cards in ascending order of their mana cost to build a combo multiplier.

### How the Combo Chain Works
1.  **Start Low**: Begin your turn with 0-cost cards, such as basic buffs or utility cards like Spinach.
2.  **Ascend**: Follow up with a 1-cost card, then a 2-cost card.
3.  **Multiplier**: Each subsequent card in the chain receives a damage or effect boost.
4.  **Wild Cards**: Utilize specific "Wild" cards to bridge gaps in your mana costs and keep the multiplier active.

| Action | Mana Cost | Multiplier Effect | Strategy Tip |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Base Play** | 0 | 1.0x | Use for setup or armor buffs |
| **First Chain** | 1 | 1.5x | Ideal for primary weapon cards |
| **Second Chain** | 2 | 2.0x+ | Save your heaviest hitters for this slot |
| **Wild Card** | Variable | Chain Reset/Hold | Use when you lack the next ascending number |

> **💡 Tip:** Playing cards out of order (e.g., a 2-cost followed by a 1-cost) will break your combo, significantly reducing your total damage output for that turn.

## Character Archetypes and Abilities

Choosing your character is the first major strategic decision you will make. Each hero offers a unique passive ability that fundamentally changes how you approach deckbuilding and the **vampire crawlers logo** visual cues in the UI.

### Antonio: The Stalwart Defender
Antonio is the go-to choice for players who prefer a "tanky" playstyle. His ability grants him Armor whenever he plays his specific character card. This allows him to survive longer in the grid-based dungeons where enemies only attack when adjacent to the player.

### Pasqualina: The Combo Specialist
Pasqualina is designed for players who want to maximize the combo system. Her ability increases the player's hand size whenever purple cards are played. A larger hand size provides more options for maintaining the ascending mana chain, making her one of the strongest characters for late-game scaling.

## Managing Deck Bloat and Card Evolutions

One of the most significant challenges in the game is "deck bloat." Every time you level up mid-fight, you are typically forced to add a new card to your deck. This can quickly dilute your strategy if you are forced to take cards that don't fit your current mana curve.

### Strategies for a Lean Deck
*   **Prioritize Synergy**: If you are playing Pasqualina, always look for purple cards to trigger her hand-size passive.
*   **Avoid Random Weapons**: Adding too many different weapon types makes it harder to draw the buffs (like Spinach) needed to start your combos.
*   **Gem Customization**: You can find gems in treasure chests to slot into cards. These gems can add double damage or extra armor.

> **⚠️ Warning:** Be cautious when adding gems to cards. In the current 2026 build, adding a gem to a card may prevent it from evolving into its stronger "Holy" or "Super" version. Plan your evolutions before committing your gems.

### Notable Card Evolutions
Evolving cards is the key to overcoming the massive health pools of late-game bosses. For example, the **Magic Wand** can evolve into the **Holy Wand**, which deals significantly more damage across multiple enemies simultaneously. These evolutions usually require a specific setup or a combination of items in your inventory.

## Meta-Progression: Permanent Upgrades

Between runs, you can spend the coins collected in dungeons on permanent upgrades. This meta-progression is vital for overcoming difficulty spikes, especially when facing enemies with high armor values.

1.  **Armor**: Increases your base damage reduction across all characters.
2.  **Luck**: Improves the quality of card rewards when leveling up, helping to mitigate deck bloat.
3.  **Healing**: Provides a percentage of health back after every successful encounter.
4.  **Greed**: Increases the amount of gold found, accelerating your overall progression.

The costs of these upgrades stack, meaning you must choose your path carefully. It is generally recommended to invest in **Luck** early to ensure your deck remains high-quality during your runs.

## Navigating the Dungeons

The dungeon crawling aspect uses a classic grid-based movement system. You move tile-by-tile, and enemies only react when you are within their immediate vicinity. This allows you to play at your own pace—either meticulously planning every movement or blasting through rooms once your build is established.

**Q: Why can't I evolve my weapons after adding gems?**

A: This is a specific design choice (or potential bug) in the 2026 version of the game. Once a card has been modified with a gem, it is often locked out of the evolution path. It is best to evolve the card first and then apply gems to the evolved version if slots are available.

**Q: What is the best way to handle "Deck Bloat"?**

A: To manage deck bloat, focus on characters like Pasqualina who increase hand size. This allows you to cycle through unwanted cards faster. Additionally, prioritize upgrades that increase "Luck" to ensure the cards you are forced to pick are at least high-quality or synergistic with your current build.

**Q: Do enemies move when I am not moving?**

A: No. The game is strictly turn-based. Enemies only take actions or move when you perform an action (moving, playing a card, or ending your turn). This gives you unlimited time to calculate your next combo.
